Artificial Intelligence Use is Exploding

There is little doubt that the widespread availability, increasing usability, and rapid refinement of generative AI models will have a significant and foundational impact on the way humans create, record, and use its collective cultural works - including our formal body of knowledge. By extension, the mechanisms of scientific research and its contribution to this corpus are also likely to change fundamentally over the next several years.

The application of AI raises many concerns, and many of them are justified in one way or another. In broad terms, these concerns are of two types: those that deal with processes and human capability to manage, describe, understand, and continue to execute these processes independently of AI, and those that pose a question mark about the content that is generated by such processes - whether it is accurate, of adequate quality, can be trusted, and are readily distinguishable from human-generated content.

Responsible Use of Artificial Intelligence in Research

Responsible use starts with the description of the creation process, and annotating content generated by AI with an identifier (an 'AiiD') that resolves to the description. Our service allows implementers of AI and ML in a research context to describe and persist the use of AI with an AiiD, and then use that persistent identifier to do the following:

check_circleCite the instance of AI use in papers and in datasets

check_circleAnnotate records in datasets, content in web pages, passages in papers, and any other uses of AI with the view to distinguishing it from human-generated content

check_circleAdd tags to web content to identify elements generated by AI

check_circleCompile a registry of the use of AI in a research context, or other facets of the landscape

Policy for AI use

Artificial intellegence, if applied responsibly, promises significant benefit for automation and scaling of research-related workflows of all kinds. It also introduces threats - including the significant danger of inability to distinguish AI-generated content from human output. This provides one the the major motivations for development and use of AIID.

Have a look at the Principles of AI Policy, developed by DANS, and make use of a template for developing your own policy.
